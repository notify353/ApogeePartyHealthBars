---
name: prepare-for-feature
description: Prepare for a forthcoming feature by orienting to the whole project and establishing a professional architectural mindset before implementation. Use only when the user explicitly asks to prepare for a new feature, establish a feature-design mindset, or invokes $prepare-for-feature; do not implement or change files during preparation.
---

Read and follow the project's instructions, then inspect enough of its architecture, current behavior, constraints, data flows, extension points, tests, and recent work to understand the big picture for the forthcoming feature; always favor a professional, coherent design with correct boundaries, simple abstractions, intuitive naming and workflows, easy extension and testing, and future developers' comprehension; never force a feature into unsuitable existing structure or settle for a patch merely to minimize changes, and explicitly recommend a foundational redesign or substantial refactor whenever that is the proper long-term solution; do not edit files or propose a design before the feature is described, and when prepared respond only: `Ready.`
